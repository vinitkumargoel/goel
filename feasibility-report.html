01

The one‑minute version

If you read nothing else, read this.

  • The engine is portable. The download brain (queue, HTTP, SFTP, HLS, checksums, history) is plain Swift that already builds for Mac and Linux. iOS is the same Swift with a new touch skin on top.
  • The core transfer app is a green light. Downloading, browsing SFTP servers, video capture, queue, speed graphs, history — all come across cleanly.
  • Some features change shape. The menu‑bar, Drop Basket and "run a script after download" become a Share button, Lock‑Screen live progress, and Shortcuts — different, often better.
  • Three things a phone can't do: seed torrents, bond multiple internet connections, and act as an always‑on server. Apple also bars torrent apps from the App Store — but Android allows them.

05

The torrent question

🧲

This is the one place iOS and Android part ways

A torrent client has to accept incoming connections and keep networking in the background to seed — both off‑limits on iOS — and Apple's App Store review routinely rejects BitTorrent apps outright. Google Play is far more permissive (plenty of torrent apps ship there), and Android allows the background service seeding needs.

iOS · App Store ≈ 20% (blocked) Android · Play ≈ 75% (allowed)

09

Watch‑outs

🍏

App Store review

Keep the store build torrent‑free and it sails through. A full‑power build lives on Android, sideloading, or EU alt‑marketplaces.

🔋

Background limits

iOS pauses long downloads when backgrounded. We lean on the system's background‑download service; very long jobs may need the app open.

📦

App size

Static‑linking libtorrent + boost is heavy. If torrents aren't in the iOS build, we drop them and stay lean.

🤖

Android engine cost

Swift doesn't run natively on Android. Budget for a Kotlin engine twin — or a shared C/Rust core — guided by the same Ports contracts.
